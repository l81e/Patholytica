About Medicinebank

Medicinebank was founded on a simple observation: the medicines people in wealthy countries discard could save lives elsewhere.

We work with hospitals, pharmacies, manufacturers, and regulators to safely collect, verify, repackage and redistribute medicines that would otherwise be destroyed. Our end-to-end chain of custody ensures full compliance with international pharmaceutical law and WHO cold-chain standards, so every recipient can trust what they receive.

How the Redistribution Chain Works

From hospital pharmacy to clinic shelf in five stages.

Donors submit medicine lots through our portal or app. Our trained volunteers collect, inspect, and verify each item against WHO quality standards. Approved stock is repackaged, logged on our traceability ledger, and shipped via our certified logistics partners to verified recipient facilities. Recipient staff confirm delivery through the app, closing the loop.

Safety & Regulatory Compliance

Medicinebank operates under full regulatory oversight in every jurisdiction we work.

We hold MHRA registration in the UK and equivalent licences in all operating countries. Every redistribution follows our Standard Operating Procedure, which has been independently audited by three international regulatory bodies. Cold-chain integrity is monitored electronically throughout transit, with full documentation available to recipients and regulators on request.

Impact Data

Our open data dashboard tracks redistribution in real time.

Since launch, Medicinebank has redistributed over 2.4 million medicine units, prevented an estimated £18 million in pharmaceutical waste, and directly supported treatment for an estimated 340,000 patients. All impact data is verified by our independent evaluation partner and published quarterly.
